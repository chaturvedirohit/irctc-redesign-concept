# IRCTC Web Redesign — Concept

An independent, AI-built **concept redesign** of India's rail booking experience — a single, self-contained HTML prototype exploring what a faster, calmer, more accessible IRCTC could feel like.

> ⚠️ **Unofficial.** This is a personal design study for learning and portfolio purposes. It is **not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to IRCTC, the Ministry of Railways, or any Government of India entity.** No real bookings, payments, or personal data are involved — all train data shown is illustrative.

## What it explores

- **Search-first home** — reserved booking leads the page; the 90% path is zero-click.
- **Full booking flow** — search → trains → fare calendar → seat/coach map → travellers → payment → ticket.
- **Plain-language status** — PNR and live-train info in words, not WL/RAC/CNF codes.
- **Waitlist confirmation predictor** — e.g. "WL 14 · 78% likely to confirm."
- **Interactive coach position** — tap a coach to see its seat layout.
- **Services** — flights, hotels, bus, metro QR tickets, food-on-train (restaurant → menu → cart), retiring rooms, cabs, refunds (TDR), platform & unreserved tickets.
- **Multilingual** — live switching between English, हिंदी and தமிழ்.
- **Accessibility-minded** — larger touch targets, senior/Divyangjan quotas, calm visual hierarchy.

## Built with

A single `index.html` file — no build step, no dependencies, no backend. Hand-built HTML, CSS, SVG and vanilla JavaScript, created iteratively with AI assistance.

## Why

India's rail platform serves a huge share of the country's travel. With a new IRCTC platform announced for 2026, this is one product manager's exploration of what the *experience* on top of that new engine could feel like. The hard problem — the high-throughput backend — is deliberately out of scope; this is a front-end concept only.
